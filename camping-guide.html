Preparation

Essential camping checklist

These basics help prevent the most common mountain-camping issues.

Shelter and comfort

  • Weather-ready tent with rainfly and groundsheet.
  • Sleeping bag rated for cool highland nights.
  • Compact blanket, extra socks, and dry clothes.

Food and hydration

  • Reusable water bottles and hydration plan.
  • Quick-cook meals, snacks, and emergency calories.
  • Trash bags for strict leave-no-trace cleanup.

Safety and navigation

  • Flashlight/headlamp and backup batteries.
  • Basic first-aid kit and personal medication.
  • Offline map screenshots and emergency contacts.

Ground Guide

Keep camp inside the marked main zone

Use the arrival gate and check-in point first, then choose a tent area inside the main grounds so access to water and sunrise viewing stays easy.

  • Arrival and check-in Unload gear early, check conditions, and avoid blocking the access path.
  • Main tent zone Use this as your default camping grounds instead of exposed side edges.
  • Water and wash area Plan cleanup here so the sleeping area stays dry and organized.
  • Sunrise ridge Walk here lightly in the morning, then return to pack out the site cleanly.
